# Roast V2: Agency, Onboarding & Branding — SimplerDevelopment

**How to use:** Run `/roast` and feed it this brief. The council judges **two lenses**: (A) does this module earn its place inside the all-in-one platform, and (B) could it stand alone as its own product. Note: the prior council issued a verdict on lens B — it cannot stand alone — and this V2 fully adopts that ruling.

## The idea

The module owns three linked jobs, but the thesis has pivoted: the prior brief claimed "propagation is the edge." The council attacked it correctly — pipes are worthless on empty tables. The real edge is **automatically acquiring and maintaining accurate brand data so downstream AI tools are never starved on day one.**

**(1) Auto-enriched onboarding:** On first login, before the wizard renders its first step, a Brandfetch API + DNS/logo scrape + CSS color-sampling call fires against the agency's own domain and pre-populates `branding_profiles` and `branding_messaging` with real colors, typography, and logo assets. The 8-step wizard then layers on top — capturing positioning, tone, mission, and feature intent that cannot be scraped — so the tables arrive at step 1 already seeded with something better than a placeholder hex. **(GO-LIVE BLOCKER — this enrichment call is not yet wired; see Constraints.)**

**(2) Brand management with a completeness score:** A full brand management system — color palette, typography, logos, button presets, tagline, elevator pitch, tone, target audience, multiple named profiles — anchored by a **brand-completeness score** surfaced visibly on the portal dashboard: "Your AI features are running at X% — add your tone to unlock full copy quality." This is the forcing function the original brief lacked: a named, visible, actionable nudge that keeps agencies returning to the branding panel rather than abandoning it post-wizard. Brand sentinel tokens (`__brand_primary__` → resolved at render via `resolveBrandSentinel()`) and 9 MCP tools (read/write brand state without panel visits) remain as architectural primitives — they already ship, already earn their keep. **(Brand-completeness score widget is not yet built; see Constraints.)**

**(3) White-label as a retention forcing-function, not a prestige feature:** Scale-tier custom domain — where a verified DNS TXT domain replaces the SD portal URL and overrides portal chrome — is reframed as the aggressive upsell that self-enforces brand setup. Agencies cannot credibly present a portal under their own brand with placeholder data inside it; the upsell naturally drives the completion behavior the wizard asks for voluntarily. **(DNS/SSL ops hardening is a committed blocker before promoting this at scale; see Constraints.)**

## Who it's for & how it makes money

- **Primary buyer:** Digital agencies using SD as their business OS who need brand context propagating to every content surface — AI copy, email campaign defaults, block themes, Company Brain context, proposal templates — and who need that context to actually be accurate from day one, not left blank until someone remembers.
- **End user:** The agency's internal team living in the branded portal; the agency's clients receiving branded deliverables (proposals, emails, site content).
- **Monetization:** Core onboarding and branding are included at all tiers — activation hook, not a revenue line. White-label / custom domain is the Scale-tier upsell, now positioned explicitly as the retention forcing-function that drives brand setup completion rather than a prestige add-on. AI copy generation burns AI credits (metered). **No standalone ambition — this is a bundled retention layer.** The prior council confirmed it has no recipients outside the suite; this V2 does not contest that finding.

## The edge

Lead claim is data quality, not pipes:

- **Auto-acquired brand data, never blank tables:** Brandfetch API + DNS/logo scrape + CSS color-sampling on signup pre-populates brand rows before the agency types a single character. The propagation architecture (brand-sentinel tokens, MCP tools, block defaults, AI copy prompts seeded from messaging fields) already ships and is genuinely rare — but the V2 thesis is that it now runs on real data by default, not on the placeholder hex codes that defeated it before.
- **Brand-completeness score as a continuous activation lever:** A dashboard widget makes brand health visible and actionable — specific per-field nudges ("add your tone to unlock AI email copy quality") turn brand setup from a one-time wizard into a continuous improvement surface. This closes the prior brief's open gap: the propagation machinery existed but had no forcing function to keep it fed.
- **White-label as the brand-setup tax that pays for itself:** Scale-tier agencies presenting SD under their own domain have a business reason to maintain accurate brand data that free-tier agencies do not. The upsell creates its own data-quality incentive without requiring SD to enforce it explicitly.
- **Brand sentinel tokens decouple content from color — and already work:** `__brand_primary__` in block JSON resolves at render time; swapping a brand profile repaints all content globally without touching individual block records. This is a real architectural primitive, not a roadmap claim.
- **MCP-exposed brand context closes the AI loop — and already ships:** 9 MCP tools let agents audit contrast, rewrite messaging to match tone, and push a new palette without a human opening the settings panel. The claim is accurate today; V2 simply adds that the tables these tools read are now seeded with real data.
- **AI theme generation: explicitly deferred.** The prior brief listed this as an edge. The council attacked it as earning skepticism rather than retention. It is not listed as a differentiator in V2 and is not on the active roadmap.

## Constraints

- Solo founder / tiny team; SD is a ~357k-line shipping monorepo (Next.js 16, Drizzle/Postgres+pgvector, Mastra agents), now open-sourcing.
- **GO-LIVE BLOCKERS — committed, scoped work being closed before any V2 pitch ships:**
  1. **Brandfetch auto-enrichment on first login** — the Brandfetch API + DNS/logo scrape + CSS color-sampling call does not yet wire into the onboarding flow. The tables currently start blank. This is a concrete engineering task (API integration + `mirrorBrandAnswers` pre-seeding step) and the single most load-bearing change in V2. Nothing in the "auto-acquired data" thesis is true until this ships.
  2. **Brand-completeness score dashboard widget** — the "running at X%" nudge and per-field prompts do not yet exist. The completeness calculation (how many required fields are non-placeholder, non-empty) and the dashboard surface both need to be built and deployed. Without this, the forcing-function claim is marketing copy, not a feature.
  3. **White-label DNS/SSL ops hardening** — DNS TXT verification, SSL provisioning, and cert lifecycle management for Scale-tier custom domains must be hardened before white-label is promoted as a mainstream upsell. Today, a cert expiry or DNS misconfiguration locks out an agency's entire team from "their own" product — a P0 incident the solo founder absorbs. This is a real operational liability. White-label is not promoted at scale until this is scoped and closed.
- Must credibly substitute or complement what agencies already use: HoneyBook, Dubsado, Bonsai (none have integrated brand management with AI propagation; Brandfetch is read-only import, not an authoring system). Brandfetch is both a competitive reference point and the data-source dependency in V2.
- Time-to-first-dollar and maintainability by a tiny team both matter. The blocker list above is sequenced: auto-enrichment ships first (highest leverage), completeness score second (closes the activation loop), DNS/SSL hardening third (unlocks Scale-tier promotion).

## Roast it on two lenses

1. **Earns its place in the suite?** Does auto-enrichment genuinely solve the data-quality problem, or will Brandfetch return stale/low-quality data for the typical agency domain and leave the tables nearly as bad as hand-entry? Does the brand-completeness score actually change agency behavior — is it a real activation lever or a dashboard metric nobody checks after week one? Is the propagation architecture (brand-sentinel tokens, AI copy seeded from messaging) a meaningful retention driver, or an invisible benefit agencies don't consciously attribute to SD?
2. **Could it stand alone?** No — the prior council's verdict stands and V2 does not contest it. **No standalone ambition — bundled retention layer.** The council should instead attack whether the bundled retention thesis is defensible at all: does brand propagation measurably increase retention over a suite that lacked it, or is it lock-in that agencies don't consciously value and therefore won't cite as a stay reason?

## Riskiest assumption to pressure-test

The original risk — that agencies never supply accurate brand data — has been structurally addressed by the auto-enrichment pivot. The new riskiest assumption is: **auto-enriched data (Brandfetch + DNS scrape + CSS color-sampling) is meaningfully better than what the median agency would enter by hand.** If the Brandfetch response for a typical agency domain returns outdated colors, a missing logo, or a tone that doesn't match the agency's current positioning, the pre-populated rows are noise rather than signal — and the downstream AI features degrade just as they did on blank tables, except now with confident-looking bad data.

The cheapest test validates or kills this in under an hour with zero build: (1) run a SQL completeness and staleness query against existing tenant `branding_profiles` / `branding_messaging` rows to establish the current baseline (how many are placeholder hex, empty tone/audience, or untouched since signup); (2) fire a Brandfetch enrichment call against the domain of one of those tenants and compare the auto-populated result against what they actually hand-entered. If auto-populated beats median manual, the V2 thesis holds and the blocker list above earns its investment. If not, the forcing-function must come from the wizard itself — better questions, required fields, inline AI suggestions — and this brief needs another pass before any code ships.
