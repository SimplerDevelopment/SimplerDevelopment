How I Think About This
The gap isn't in execution. It's in understanding the actual problem.

Most companies know where they want to go.

Very few know what's actually going to get them there.

Take a website.
When someone says "it's not working," that usually means one of three things:
  • Not enough people are finding it
  • People find it but don't take action
  • People don't understand what you offer or why it matters
Three different problems. Three completely different solutions. Most teams pick one and start spending. Strategy tells you which one you actually have. Before the spending starts. That applies to messaging, campaigns, audiences, and channels.
CY Strategies
What Strategy Actually Does
A strategy isn't a plan. It's a set of decisions that make a plan worth following.

Most teams can tell you what they're going to do. Very few can tell you why those things and not something else.

Good strategy answers the questions most teams skip. Who exactly are we trying to reach? What has to be true before they act? Which message, which channel, at which moment?

Without those answers, a plan is just activity. Strategy is the thinking that makes the activity matter.
