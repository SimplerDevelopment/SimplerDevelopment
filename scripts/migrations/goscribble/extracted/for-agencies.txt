===== SECTION class="problem-bg" =====
H2: The further from the bedside, the more you lose.
  P: Your clinician finishes a visit at 10am. By the time they sit down to chart at 8pm, the nuances are gone. The observation that would have changed a reimbursement tier, the symptom detail that mattered for the care plan, the exact functional score that QA will flag. That gap is costing your agency more than you think.
H3: Lost reimbursement you earned
  P: OASIS scores documented from memory hours later are systematically lower than what was observed. Inaccurate functional and clinical scores mean lower PDGM payment tiers, on every episode.
H3: QA rework that shouldn't exist
  P: Most OASIS errors aren't carelessness; they're recall errors. A clinician who can't remember exactly what they observed fills in fields with their best guess. Your QA team fixes it. Or doesn't catch it.
H3: Compliance risk hiding in plain sight
  P: Every OASIS field that can't be traced back to a documented clinical observation is an audit vulnerability. The gap between what happened and what was written is where compliance exposure lives.
H3: Clinicians who eventually leave
  P: When your best nurses are charting at 9pm, they're not just tired; they're telling you something. The agencies losing clinicians to burnout are the ones asking them to carry the visit in their head all day.
H3: Billing delays you can't afford
  P: Incomplete documentation at visit close delays your 485 orders, which delays your billing cycle. Every day of delay is cash you've already earned sitting idle.
H3: Capacity you can't unlock
  P: A clinician spending 3 hours on documentation after visits can't take on more patients. That's not a staffing problem; it's a tools problem. The capacity is there. The time isn't.

  P: Documentation pushes directly to your existing system. No new logins, no double entry.

  P: When documentation is captured at the bedside, every downstream process, billing, coding, QA, care planning, is built on what actually happened, not what someone recalled.
